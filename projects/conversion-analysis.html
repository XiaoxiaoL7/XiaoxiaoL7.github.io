Project 02
A/B Testing · Product Analytics

Conversion Rate &
A/B Testing Analysis

End-to-end A/B testing analysis to evaluate the impact of a product presentation change on conversion and revenue metrics. Used Python and SQL to compare control and treatment groups, validate statistical significance and communicate findings through a dashboard.

Tools: Python · SQL · Statistical testing
Type: Independent project
Context: Food delivery platform
01 — Overview
The problem

On a food delivery platform, the product team proposed a change to how menu items were presented to customers — a redesigned item card with larger images and clearer pricing. The business question was: does this presentation change actually improve the customer's likelihood of adding an item to their cart and completing an order?

Rather than rolling it out immediately, I designed an A/B test to measure the causal effect of this change on conversion rate and revenue per session, with rigorous statistical validation to avoid false conclusions.

02 — Methodology
How the test was designed

I used a two-sample proportion test (z-test) with a pre-registered significance threshold of α = 0.05 and target statistical power of 80%. Sample size was calculated before running the test to ensure the experiment was adequately powered to detect a meaningful effect.

04 — Results
Findings & recommendations
Treatment significantly outperformed
+18% conversion lift, statistically significant at p < 0.01. Reject H0 with high confidence.
Mobile users drove the largest effect
+24% lift on mobile vs +11% on desktop — larger images had more impact on smaller screens.
Revenue impact confirmed
+12% revenue per session in treatment group. Higher conversion with no reduction in average order value.
Recommendation: full rollout
Estimated annualised revenue uplift of 8–14% based on observed effect, justified the development cost.
