Predicting Business Performance
Using News Sentiment

Applied FinBERT sentiment scoring to financial news and social media data for 65 S&P 100 companies, then used these sentiment signals alongside financial ratios in a neural network model to predict quarterly business performance indicators — ROE, current ratio, profit margin and stock price change rate.

Tools: Python · FinBERT · Neural network · Pandas
Data: 65 companies · 2019–2022 · quarterly
Grade: Cum laude
01 — Overview
The research question

Financial news and social media carry forward-looking signals about firm performance that traditional financial ratios alone may not capture. This thesis investigated whether adding FinBERT-scored sentiment from two sources — social media and Orbis financial news — to a model already trained on financial ratios would improve the accuracy of predicting next-quarter business performance.

The model used three consecutive quarters of financial data and sentiment scores to predict the fourth quarter across four performance indicators. Four experimental scenarios were compared to isolate the contribution of each sentiment source.

02 — Methodology
Experimental design

Each of the four financial indicators (ROE, current ratio, profit margin, stock price quarterly change) was used as the prediction target while the remaining three served as inputs. FinBERT sentiment scores from two sources were added incrementally across four experimental scenarios to measure their contribution to model accuracy.

04 — Results
Key findings
Orbis news beats social media for ROE
Orbis news sentiment alone reduced ROE prediction MAE by 42% (1.14 → 0.66). Social media alone: 38% reduction. News-based sentiment carries stronger signal for fundamental performance.
Combined sources best for profit margin
For profit margin prediction, using both sentiment sources together gave the best result — MAE reduced 20% (1.10 → 0.88). Neither source alone matched this.
Stock price harder to predict
Sentiment improved stock price prediction less consistently — market prices incorporate many factors beyond the sentiment captured in news. Social media sentiment helped slightly (MAE: 0.98 → 0.91).
Sentiment is a meaningful signal
In 3 of 4 indicators, at least one sentiment source improved prediction accuracy. The finding supports using NLP-extracted sentiment as a supplementary input to financial forecasting models.
